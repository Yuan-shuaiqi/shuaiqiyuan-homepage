Shuaiqi Yuan(原帅琪)

I am dedicated to developing quantitative models for reliability, risk, and resilience assessment of engineering systems. My research interests include industrial cyber-physical systems, chemical process safety, safety & security integration, dynamic risks, uncertainty quantification, uncertainty-informed machine learning, and decision-making under uncertainty.

I have published around thirty peer-review papers and several book chapters associated with process safety and security. A full publication list can be found in my Google Scholar: https://scholar.google.com/citations?user=QwgENusAAAAJ&hl=en. I am also a reviewer for around twenty international journals in the safety science and risk science domain, such as Safety Science, Reliability Engineering & System Safety, Risk Analysis, etc.

Educational Background

  • Ph.D. Degree in Safety and Security Science, Delft University of Technology, 2024
  • M.Eng. Degree in Safety Engineering, China University of Mining and Technology, Beijing, 2020
  • B.Eng. Degree in Mining Engineering, China University of Mining and Technology, Beijing, 2017

Research Highlights/Interests

  • Probabilistic risk assessment - quantitative risk modeling of engineering systems, such as industrial control systems and urban critical infrastructures.
  • Quantitative reliability and resilience modeling - quantitative reliability/resilience modeling of engineering systems for the sake of safety or security.
  • AI for safety & security - leverage AI algorithms to lift safety and security performance of engineering systems.
  • Uncertainty quantification of machine learning - safety assurance of ML models; risk-aware ML for mission-critical decision settings.
  • Decision-making under uncertainty - risk-based and uncertainty-informed decision-making pertinent to safety and security assurance.
